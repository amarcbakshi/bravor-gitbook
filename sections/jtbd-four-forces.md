# Jobs to Be Done: Four Forces

## The Core Job

"Connect my professional skills to an organization that urgently needs them — so my 10 hours this month creates real impact instead of disappearing into a generic volunteer database."

The stickiest part of this job is not functional — it is emotional. The professional needs to believe three things: (1) my specific expertise matters here, (2) this organization is legitimate and effective, and (3) my time won't be wasted.

### Functional Jobs

- Find a vetted organization that needs my specific skills right now
- Understand exactly what the task requires before committing (scope, hours, deadline)
- Contribute on my schedule — not theirs
- See the connection between my work and the organization's outcomes
- Build a track record of meaningful contributions

### Emotional Jobs

- Stop feeling guilty about not doing enough
- Replace helplessness with agency — channel anxiety about democratic decline into concrete action
- Feel that my professional identity is an asset, not irrelevant to the causes I care about
- Experience the satisfaction of solving a hard problem (a legal brief, a media strategy) for an organization whose mission I believe in
- Belong to a community of professionals who contribute — not just a donor list

### Social Jobs

- Be known as someone who contributes expertise, not just money
- Join a peer network of professionals who take civic engagement seriously
- Signal competence and values without being politically performative
- Build professional relationships with mission-aligned colleagues across sectors

## The Four Forces

### Push Forces (away from current behavior)

| Force | Strength | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Frustration with generic volunteering | Strong | Professionals report that available opportunities don't match their skills<sup>1</sup> |
| Anxiety about democratic decline | Strong | Post-2024 civic engagement surge across professional communities |
| Guilt about inaction | Moderate | Professionals express desire to contribute but cite lack of pathway |
| Awareness of nonprofit capacity gaps | Moderate | Media coverage of understaffed organizations, legal defense shortfalls |

### Pull Forces (toward Bravor)

| Force | Strength | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Skill-matched opportunities | Strong | "I want to use my actual expertise" — the #1 unmet need in volunteering surveys<sup>2</sup> |
| Flexible time commitment | Strong | 5–20 hours/month matches professional availability patterns |
| Verified, high-impact organizations | Strong | Trust infrastructure removes the biggest barrier to engagement |
| Impact visibility | Moderate | Seeing your contribution connected to outcomes drives retention |

### Habit Forces (inertia keeping them in place)

| Force | Strength | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| "I'll just donate money instead" | Moderate | Bravor's framing: "Your skills are worth $250/hour — donate those" |
| Comfort with inaction | Moderate | AI onboarding makes the first step conversational, not bureaucratic |
| Existing volunteer commitments | Low | Bravor's fractional model doesn't compete with existing commitments |

### Anxiety Forces (concerns about switching)

| Force | Strength | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Time waste | Strong | Task scoping with clear hours, deadlines, and deliverables |
| Privacy and exposure | Moderate | Profile visibility controls; anonymous until matched |
| Professional reputation risk | Moderate | Vetted organizations only; code of conduct on both sides |
| Quality of organizations | Moderate | Verification process and feedback ratings build trust |

## Key Insight

The balance of forces is overwhelmingly favorable. Push forces are strong and growing (democratic anxiety, professional guilt). Pull forces are direct and addressable (skill matching, flexibility, trust). The primary anxiety — time waste — is mitigated by the platform's core design (scoped tasks, verified orgs, clear deliverables).

The tipping point for adoption is **the first successful match.** Once a professional completes one task and sees the impact, retention rates climb sharply. The product must be optimized for speed-to-first-match above all else.
