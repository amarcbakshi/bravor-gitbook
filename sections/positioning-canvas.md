# Positioning Canvas

## Market Category

**The AI-enabled volunteer matching platform for skilled professionals and pro-democracy organizations.**

This category does not formally exist yet. The closest analogy: LinkedIn created "professional networking" by making it obvious that career connections should be systematized. Bravor creates "skilled civic matching" by making it obvious that professional expertise should flow to the organizations that need it most — automatically, not accidentally.

## Competitive Alternatives

When a professional feels the impulse to contribute their skills, these are the approaches they actually consider:

| Approach | What it represents | Win rate |
|---|---|---|
| Do nothing | The impulse arises and dissipates. No clear pathway exists. | ~70–80% |
| Generic volunteer platforms | VolunteerMatch, Catchafire, Idealist. Not skill-matched or flexible enough. | ~5–10% |
| DIY networking | Ask friends, search LinkedIn, email organizations cold. High friction. | ~5–10% |
| Political mobilization | Campaign volunteering, phone banking, rally attendance. Doesn't use professional skills. | ~5–10% |
| Freelance/consulting | Upwork, Fiverr, Catalant. Skill-matched but transactional and not mission-driven. | ~2–5% |

**Key insight:** The primary competitor is inertia — the absence of a trusted, efficient pathway from "I want to help" to "I'm helping." The competitive battle is Bravor vs. the locked gate between intention and action.

## Five Distinct Capabilities

- **AI conversational onboarding** — builds richer profiles than any form-based system. Captures nuance in expertise, availability patterns, and mission orientation through natural dialogue.

- **Skill-to-need matching** — doesn't just match "lawyer" to "legal task." Matches immigration law expertise to an immigration rights org's contract review need, on a timeline that fits the attorney's Wednesday afternoons.

- **Fractional-time design** — the entire platform is built for professionals with 5–20 hours per month, not full-time volunteers. Tasks are scoped, time-bound, and flexible.

- **Mission-aligned vetting** — both sides are verified. Organizations for impact and credibility. Professionals for credentials and reliability. This creates a trust layer that generic platforms lack.

- **White-label institutional infrastructure** — bar associations, university clinics, and media networks license the matching engine to mobilize their own communities. This is both a distribution channel and a defensible moat.

## Three Value Themes

| Value theme | What the professional gets | What the organization gets |
|---|---|---|
| "Your skills, not your time" | Contribute expertise at the level you're trained for — not generic volunteer work | Access specialized skills without recruitment overhead or consultant fees |
| "Flexible, not forever" | 10 hours this month, 5 next month, none in August. The platform adapts. | Predictable, scoped contributions that fit project timelines |
| "Trusted connections" | Every organization is vetted. Every task is real. Your time matters here. | Every contributor is verified. Quality and reliability are built into the system. |

## Best-Fit Customer

The ideal first contributor has five characteristics:

- **Mid-career professional** (5–15 years experience) in law, media, finance, design, or strategy
- **Fractional availability** — has 5–20 hours per month, not full-time capacity
- **Tried to help before** — searched for opportunities, maybe volunteered once, found nothing that matched their skills
- **Mission-aligned but not activist** — cares about democracy and rights but doesn't identify as a political organizer
- **Values efficiency** — will engage immediately with a clear, well-scoped task; will disengage instantly from a vague or time-wasting platform
