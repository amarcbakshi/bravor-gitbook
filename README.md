# Test Overview Brief

## The matching layer for skilled volunteering in the pro-democracy movement

The pro-democracy ecosystem has an infrastructure problem. Thousands of skilled professionals — attorneys, designers, media strategists, financial analysts — have time and expertise they want to contribute. Meanwhile, frontline organizations fighting for democracy, immigrant rights, reproductive rights, and civil liberties desperately need specialized skills they can't afford to hire. Both sides are looking for each other. Neither can find the other.

A legal expert in Minnesota doesn't know the immigrant rights organization down the street needs a contract reviewed. A media strategist with weekend hours discovers no system exists to route her contribution toward a journalist facing censure. An organization facing a legal deadline has no way to surface the attorney ready to help. The workarounds — Slack channels, email lists, personal networks — leak opportunities at every step.

Bravor fixes this. It is an AI-enabled matching platform that connects skilled professionals with vetted frontline organizations for discrete, high-impact volunteer work.

## How it works

Both sides onboard through a conversational AI assistant that builds rich profiles — capturing expertise, availability, mission orientation, and specific needs. A matching engine then routes concrete, time-bound opportunities (legal review, financial modeling, press strategy, op-ed editing) to the professionals whose skills and schedules align. Organizations post what they need. Professionals see what matches. Vetted connections happen in hours, not weeks.

## What makes this different

No existing platform serves this intersection:

| Existing approach | What's missing |
|---|---|
| Catchafire / VolunteerMatch | Require long-term commitments; no AI matching |
| DemocracyLab / Code for America | Tech skills only — no legal, media, finance, strategy |
| Upwork / Fiverr | Transactional and market-rate; not mission-driven |
| Personal networks | Don't scale; leak opportunities; exclude newcomers |

Bravor is the first platform that combines mission-aligned vetting, AI-enabled skill matching, and flexible time commitments across multiple professional domains.

## The scale opportunity

The platform launches with a legal-first wedge — connecting attorneys and paralegals with litigation-heavy nonprofits — then expands into media, finance, design, and strategy. Institutional partners (bar associations, university legal clinics, media rights nonprofits) can license the matching infrastructure to mobilize their own member networks, creating a sustainable scale engine.

Stage 1 targets 50–100 contributors and 15–20 partner organizations with a $500K investment over 6 months. A working prototype is already live — see [Live Prototype](sections/live-prototype.md).
